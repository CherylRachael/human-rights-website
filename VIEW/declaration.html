The Universal Declaration of Human Rights

The Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR)

The Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR) was adopted by the United Nations General Assembly in 1948 and consists of a preamble and 30 articles.

Preamble

Whereas recognition of the inherent dignity and of the equal and inalienable rights of all members of the human family is the foundation of freedom, justice and peace in the world, Whereas disregard and contempt for human rights have resulted in barbarous acts which have outraged the conscience of mankind, and the advent of a world in which human beings shall enjoy freedom of speech and belief and freedom from fear and want has been proclaimed as the highest aspiration of the common people, Whereas it is essential, if man is not to be compelled to have recourse, as a last resort, to rebellion against tyranny and oppression, that human rights should be protected by the rule of law, Whereas it is essential to promote the development of friendly relations between nations, Whereas the peoples of the United Nations have in the Charter reaffirmed their faith in fundamental human rights, in the dignity and worth of the human person and in the equal rights of men and women and have determined to promote social progress and better standards of life in larger freedom, Whereas Member States have pledged themselves to achieve, in co-operation with the United Nations, the promotion of universal respect for and observance of human rights and fundamental freedoms, Now, therefore, The General Assembly proclaims this Universal Declaration of Human Rights as a common standard of achievement for all peoples and all nations, to the end that every individual and every organ of society, keeping this Declaration constantly in mind, shall strive by teaching and education to promote respect for these rights and freedoms and by progressive measures, national and international, to secure their universal and effective recognition and observance, both among the peoples of Member States themselves and among the peoples of territories under their jurisdiction.

Article Summaries

  • Article 1: All human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights. Definition: This means everyone has the same rights and is treated equally.
  • Article 2: Everyone is entitled to all rights and freedoms without distinction of any kind. Definition: No one can be denied their rights based on nationality, gender, or any other status.
  • Article 3: Everyone has the right to life, liberty, and security of person. Definition: This means everyone has the right to live in freedom and safety.
  • Article 4: No one shall be held in slavery or servitude. Definition: This means no one can be enslaved or forced to work against their will.
  • Article 5: No one shall be subjected to torture or to cruel, inhuman, or degrading treatment or punishment. Definition: This means everyone has the right to be treated humanely and not be tortured.
  • Article 6: Everyone has the right to recognition everywhere as a person before the law. Definition: This means everyone is recognized as a person with rights by the law.
  • Article 7: All are equal before the law and are entitled without any discrimination to equal protection of the law. Definition: This means the law protects everyone equally without discrimination.
  • Article 8: Everyone has the right to an effective remedy by the competent national tribunals for acts violating their fundamental rights. Definition: This means everyone can seek justice through the courts if their rights are violated.
  • Article 9: No one shall be subjected to arbitrary arrest, detention, or exile. Definition: This means no one can be arrested, detained, or exiled without a fair reason.
  • Article 10: Everyone is entitled in full equality to a fair and public hearing by an independent and impartial tribunal. Definition: This means everyone has the right to a fair trial by an impartial court.
  • Article 11: Everyone charged with a penal offence has the right to be presumed innocent until proven guilty. Definition: This means everyone is considered innocent until proven guilty.
  • Article 12: No one shall be subjected to arbitrary interference with their privacy, family, home, or correspondence. Definition: This means everyone's privacy and home life are protected by law.
  • Article 13: Everyone has the right to freedom of movement and residence within the borders of each state. Definition: This means everyone can travel and live anywhere within their country.
  • Article 14: Everyone has the right to seek and to enjoy in other countries asylum from persecution. Definition: This means people can seek refuge in another country if they are persecuted.
  • Article 15: Everyone has the right to a nationality. Definition: This means everyone has the right to belong to a country.
  • Article 16: Men and women of full age have the right to marry and to found a family. Definition: This means everyone has the right to marry and start a family.
  • Article 17: Everyone has the right to own property alone as well as in association with others. Definition: This means everyone can own property by themselves or with others.
  • Article 18: Everyone has the right to freedom of thought, conscience, and religion. Definition: This means everyone can think, believe, and practice their religion freely.
  • Article 19: Everyone has the right to freedom of opinion and expression. Definition: This means everyone can express their opinions and ideas freely.
  • Article 20: Everyone has the right to freedom of peaceful assembly and association. Definition: This means everyone can meet and form groups peacefully.
  • Article 21: Everyone has the right to take part in the government of their country. Definition: This means everyone can participate in their country's government.
  • Article 22: Everyone has the right to social security. Definition: This means everyone is entitled to social and economic support.
  • Article 23: Everyone has the right to work, to free choice of employment, and to just and favorable conditions of work. Definition: This means everyone has the right to work in fair conditions and choose their job freely.
  • Article 24: Everyone has the right to rest and leisure, including reasonable limitation of working hours. Definition: This means everyone is entitled to rest and reasonable work hours.
  • Article 25: Everyone has the right to a standard of living adequate for health and well-being. Definition: This means everyone is entitled to a decent standard of living for their health and well-being.
  • Article 26: Everyone has the right to education. Definition: This means everyone has the right to go to school and receive an education.
  • Article 27: Everyone has the right freely to participate in the cultural life of the community. Definition: This means everyone can freely participate in their community's cultural activities.
  • Article 28: Everyone is entitled to a social and international order in which the rights and freedoms set forth in this Declaration can be fully realized. Definition: This means everyone is entitled to a social and international environment where these rights can be fully achieved.
  • Article 29: Everyone has duties to the community in which alone the free and full development of their personality is possible. Definition: This means everyone has responsibilities to their community for their full development.
  • Article 30: Nothing in this Declaration may be interpreted as implying for any State, group, or person any right to engage in any activity aimed at the destruction of any of the rights and freedoms set forth herein. Definition: This means no one can use this Declaration to destroy the rights and freedoms it contains.
